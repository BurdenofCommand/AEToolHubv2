## Introduction

Astro Empires is a Massively Multiplayer Online Game (MMOG) of space strategy.

Players can build bases, space fleets, research new technologies, fight battles, make alliances, and much more. Each player starts from a single astro, and can expand into a full-fledged empire from there.

---

## Map

Each server on Astro Empires has many galaxies. Each galaxy has hundreds of stars and thousands of planets, moons and asteroids. No two galaxies are the same; each is different from the others.

A galaxy is divided into 100 square regions, laid out in a 10 x 10 grid. Each region can contain stars; some regions have several dozen stars, while others only have a few (and sometimes none at all).

Each star on the map represents a solar system, which can have several astros (a term used to refer to planets, moons, and other objects that orbit stars). Most astros can support bases, which allow a player to expand their empire. Only one base can be on any given astro, but bases can be expanded and improved by building various structures. All astros have certain common characteristics, such as terrain type, construction area, solar energy, fertility and various resources (namely metal, gas, and crystals).

A player can only see detailed information about bases or fleets owned by other players if they have a base or fleet somewhere in that region of space, or if one of their guild members has a base or fleet in that star system.

### Wormholes (AE v4)

A wormhole is a portal that creates a shortcut, allowing for fast travel.

In each galaxy, there are 2 randomly placed wormholes with fixed positions — one located centrally (regions 44/45/54/55) and the other placed in the outer regions.

Players can use the wormholes to travel to any location with a speed boost. By using wormholes, stellar units can travel to other galaxies. Death Stars are not able to use wormholes.

Wormholes provide a speed factor of **1 + 0.5*n**, where n is the floor(average of the top 5 server Jump Gates). For example, if the average top 5 Jump Gates is level 2, then the Wormhole speed factor becomes 1+0.5*2 = 2, meaning the fleet will move 2x the normal speed.

In newly opened galaxy clusters, the wormhole speed bonus depends on that cluster's Jump Gates until fleets can move from older to new clusters, then all server Jump Gates count.

To use a wormhole, the player must place the desired fleet at the location of the wormhole and select the option "Use Wormhole." It's not possible to build bases at wormhole locations (except on the Axion server).

## Coordinates

Astro Empires uses a specialized coordinate system to denote locations (all numbers separated by colons):

1. **Galaxy** — the initial of the server followed by a number representing the specific galaxy. E.g., server Alpha, first galaxy = `A00`.
2. **Region** — row and column of the galaxy it's in. E.g., row 5, column 1 = `A00:51`.
3. **System** — row and column of the region it's in. E.g., top-right corner of the region = `A00:51:09`.
4. **Astro** — star orbit number + planetary orbit number. E.g., first planet = `10` (first star orbit, first planetary orbit); moons around it = `11`, `12`, `13`, etc. First planet full address: `A00:51:09:10`.

## Credits

Credits are the monetary unit used in Astro Empires, and are the basis of the game's economy. Credits are used to build structures and defenses on bases, to set up trade routes, to produce fleet (ships), and to invest in researching new technologies.

Credits are received every hour on the hour, based on the total economy of a player's empire. Three main methods of increasing credits gained:

- Building structures to improve an individual base's economy
- Establishing trade routes between bases
- Occupying the bases of another player

Additional credit sources: recycling debris left at an astro after ships are destroyed, plundering trade routes, pillaging conquered bases, and scrapping ships or base structures.

The main goal of Astro Empires is to optimize the resources at each base to efficiently improve them and expand one's empire — by conquering other bases or building new ones — to increase credit income. Diplomacy and treaties (Alliances, Mutual Defense Pacts) are good ways to achieve this.

## Bases

Having a base on an astro means a player owns that astro, and can use it to build structures that boost construction, production, and research, and improve economy (credit income).

Players can build defensive structures to protect a base, shipyards to produce starships, and research labs to research technologies, among others.

All structures cost credits (affecting build time) and require energy. Most structures require one "area" and one "population" unit per level, with exceptions (e.g., Urban Structures don't require population; Terraforming and Orbital Bases require neither area nor population).

Defenses automatically help protect a base from attackers and protect that player's ships in orbit — but not the fleets of guild members in orbit.

**Build time formula:** credit cost of structure ÷ base's construction rating (credits/hour). E.g., a new base builds at 12 credits/hour; a Metal Refinery costs 1 credit initially → 1/12 hour (5 minutes) to build.

Fleets (ships) can only be produced at bases with shipyards. Ships have a credit cost and build time (based on production rating), require multiple shipyards, and have tech requirements.

Technologies can only be researched at bases with research labs, with cost and time based on research rating.

New bases can only be built by constructing outpost ships and sending them to empty astros — scout first to avoid wasting an outpost ship.

A player can conquer other players' bases (occupying them), receiving 30% of the base's income, but not controlling it directly. The occupied base suffers a 30% reduction to construction, research, and production ratings.

---

## Technologies

Researching new technologies primarily increases the capacities of a player's ships and bases. They also unlock new structures and starships, and grant access to more advanced technologies.

Technologies can only be researched at bases with research labs — nearly all technologies require multiple research labs.

## Trade

Once a base has at least one spaceport, it can start a trade route with another base.

**Setup cost** = 2 × distance between the two bases. E.g., a route between bases 10 lightyears apart costs 20 credits. If set between two different players, both pay half.

**Trade Income** = Sqrt(Lowest base's income) × [1 + Sqrt(2×Distance)/75 + Sqrt(Players)/10], where Players = number of players in the trade network.

A base can have one trade route per 5 levels of spaceports (level 1, 5, 10, etc.).

Trade routes can be plundered by other players, terminating the route and giving the plunderer profit equal to the route's cost.

**Closing trade routes:** when canceled, each partner gets a 50% refund; the route enters "Closing" status for 12 hours (under 1000 distance) or 24 hours (longer). Pillaging a base yields 50% of the cost of any closing trade routes, which are then terminated.

---

## Fleets

Fleets are the main way players attack (and defend against) other players. Ships are built at shipyards; a fleet is a group of ships. Fleets can protect a base or attack another player's fleets/bases.

Fleets can be moved to other astros. Fleets from several players at the same location will merge automatically once both arrive. No fleet auto-attacks — launching requires selecting the fleet, "Attack," then the target.

**Detection time (hrs)** = 24×((-200000/((Size×1.6×0.9^Stealth tech)+200000))+1) + 1/120 — applies to fleets from players outside your guild.

**Fleet Limit:** Maximum Supported Fleet Size = Total Production × 2500. Once reached, no new units can be produced until production capacity increases.

## Attacks

A successful attack on a base does two things:

1. Allows the attacker to occupy the base, transferring 30% of the base's credit income to the occupier (tribute). No structures are destroyed by an attack; base defenses have an effectiveness rating that drops to 0% once occupied, regenerating 1%/hour after occupation ends.
2. Gives the attacker some of the defender's credits (pillage).

**Pillage formulas:**
- Base not occupied/pillaged in last 100 hours: `Pillage = 2 × Base Economy × Owner's base count`
- Base occupied/pillaged recently: `Pillage = (hours since last pillage; max 100)% × Base Economy × 8`
- NPC bases: `Pillage = (hours since last pillage; max 100)% × Base Economy × 16`
- Additional Pillage bonus: `(base actual income − new income)^2 × 5`

No base can be pillaged more than once every 24 hours; further attacks in that window occupy but don't pillage. An occupied base has 30% less construction, production, and research capacity.

If ships are destroyed in an attack, 20% of the cost of destroyed units is given to each player as combat loot, and 40% becomes debris at the attack location (collectible via recyclers, converted to credits hourly).

### Unrest

Occupied/pillaged bases (within last 24h) gain +10% unrest/day. Otherwise unrest decreases 10%/day (min 0%). At 100% unrest, the owner gets a Revolt button to free the base. While unrest is ≥50%, the base can't be newly occupied (though it and fleet on it can still be attacked, without pillage). If unrest is ≥50% and non-owner/non-guild fleets are present, the base's Jump Gates are disabled. When unrest drops from 50% to 40%, defenses fully restore and Jump Gates re-enable.

## Combat

**Basics:**
- **Attack** = damage a unit deals.
- **Armour** = damage a unit can take before dying.
- **Shielding** = prevents damage equal to itself per shot (1% of attack always bypasses shields, except Ion weapons where 50% bypasses shielding).

**Example:** A Cruiser dealing 40 damage against a Titan with 30 shielding only deals 10 damage. A Titan with 4000 armour would need 400 Cruisers to one-shot kill it (simplified, ignoring the 1% cross-shield damage).

For Cruiser-class ships and larger, damage insufficient to kill leaves a damaged remnant requiring 50% of build cost to repair, scaled by damage percentage (e.g., a Battleship at 0.6 damage costs 400 credits to repair).

Base defenses auto-repair at 1%/hour in non-occupied bases.

Max fighters that can target a unit per combat round = 2 × unit size.

---

## Experience

After combat, players gain XP based on total destruction (both sides) × a modifier based on level difference.

- If enemy level > your level: `XP = (enemy/your level)/20 × Total Fleet Destroyed` (max 10% × total fleet destroyed)
- If your level > enemy level: `XP = (enemy/your level)^2/20 × Total Fleet Destroyed`
- Combat with NPCs: `XP = 5% × Total Fleet Destroyed`

Total combat XP doesn't directly measure skill — it reflects involvement in more/larger battles against stronger opponents, and incentivizes fighting at or above your own level. XP can be spent on commanders.

## Newbies

Newbie protection rules:

- Level < 10 — bases can't be attacked.
- Level ≥ 10 — bases can only be attacked by players within 150% of the defender's level.
- Protections apply only to bases, not to fleets outside bases or unguarded trade routes.
- Protections don't apply in occupied bases if the attacker is the occupier or same guild.
- If a protected player attacks others beyond the protection threshold outside their bases, they lose protection for 48 hours.

If a player attacks/occupies someone below 65% of their level, they only receive x% of pillage/trade plunder/occupation income, where `x% = Defender level / Attacker level × 100%`.

If surrounded by stronger players, joining a guild ASAP is recommended for protection.

## Empire

A player's empire = all of that player's bases and fleet, plus all bases occupied by that player.

Three main strength measures: total economy, total fleet, total technological development (fleet/tech measured in credits spent).

**Player Level** = (Economy × 100 + Fleet + Technology) ^ 0.25 (4th root)

---

## Guild

A guild is a group of players working together under one banner, run by a guildmaster; other players can hold roles (e.g., diplomacy). Guilds can be democratic, anarchistic, totalitarian, etc. Guilds provide protection and mutual support.

Guild members have access to a guild board for communication, and guilds can maintain their own website/forum.

Players can create or apply to join a guild. The guildmaster accepts members, can kick members (members can also leave anytime), and can appoint a new guildmaster or grant permissions to members. Guild members cannot attack each other or interfere with each other's fleets/planets.

**Guild Level** = (Guild Economy × 100 + Guild Fleet + Avg. Guild Technology) ^ 0.25 (4th root)

## Commanders

Commanders can be recruited and trained (using XP or credits) for specific bonuses.

- A base can only have one base commander at a time, providing a bonus based on skill and level.
- When a base is pillaged or successfully attacked, each commander there has a 10% chance of being killed. Commanders not on duty can also die.
- A construction commander can't be removed while a structure is under construction at that base (same for research/production commanders during active tasks).
- Once assigned to a base, a commander can't be reassigned for at least 48 hours.
- Requires at least 1 command center to recruit a commander. Max commanders = level of Computer technology.
- Commanders in the pool take 10 minutes to reach any base; moving between bases takes the same time as a scout ship's travel.

## Upgrading

Upgrading removes advertising and adds extra features. Costs less than €0.06/day on a 12-month plan. Compare free vs. upgraded accounts on the Account/Upgrade page.

Accepted payment methods: PayPal, credit card, wire transfer, Western Union, phone (DaoPay). Players can upgrade other players' accounts via their profiles. New accounts start upgraded for the first 7 days.

Upgrading funds servers, personnel, and further development — supporting the long-term success of the game.

## Vacation Mode

Rules:

- Only upgraded players can set vacation mode.
- Can't be removed for 72 hours, except within the first hour it's set.
- After removal, attacks can only start again after 4 hours.
- Can only be reactivated 5 days after removal.
- Usable for a max of 35 days per civil year.

While active:

- Bases can't be attacked (effective 24 hours after being set).
- Fleets outside bases and on occupied bases can still be attacked.
- Occupied bases can still be attacked/pillaged.
- Profile shows a vacation badge (effective after 24 hours).
- Player receives half economy income, even after 4 days of inactivity.
- No fleet movement or other fleet actions.
- Recyclers stop collecting debris.
- No new construction, production, research, or trade routes can be started/queued.
- Items already queued stay queued, but completion time doubles.
- Guild mates can't use the player's Jump Gates.

## Starting Guide

If you've just started, other players may already be far stronger — don't worry, the game protects new bases against stronger players early on.

1. Raise base construction first — build a few **Metal Refineries** (improves construction/production and economy).
2. Around Metal Refinery level 4, costs rise — build a **Research Lab** and research **Computer** to level 2 to unlock **Robotic Factories**; alternate building both.
3. Research **Energy** to level 6 to unlock **Fusion Plants** (better than Solar/Gas Plants, unless on a planet with solar/gas energy 4).
4. Start building some **Shipyards** early — cheap and they boost economy.
5. When credits run low, raise Economy: build **Spaceports** and set up a **Trade Route** (ask permission first; prefer a guildmate).
6. **Crystal Mines** also boost economy, if your planet has Crystals.
7. To expand, build **Outpost Ships**. Requirements per Game Tables: Warp Drive (1), which needs Research Labs (8), Stellar Drive (4), Energy (8), and Shipyards (8) to build the Outpost Ship. Achievable in under a day with full queues.
8. Move the Outpost Ship to a chosen astro and select "Build Base" from the Fleet Menu (first new base costs 100 credits; price increases with each additional base).
9. Grow the second base faster than the first, watch for conquerors, and start building defenses.
10. Join a guild as early as possible for protection.

## Occupied Guide

Simple strategy when your bases are occupied:

1. Build lots of command centers — the more, the better.
2. Match your unit build to counter the occupier: unshielded craft → fighters; cruisers/heavy cruisers → bombers/heavy bombers; battleships → heavy bombers; very large units → ion bombers.
3. Don't attack on a predictable schedule.
4. Use recyclers to collect debris, but don't leave them stationed there.
5. Occupied bases gain +10% unrest/day; at 100% you can Revolt to free the base. At ≥50% unrest, no new occupation is possible. When unrest drops from 50% to 40%, defenses fully restore — so keep building defenses to resist re-occupation.

If you do well, the occupier's costs will outweigh their gains, increasing the chance they withdraw. Joining a good guild for help, and diplomacy with a stronger opponent, are both valuable. As a last resort, disbanding bases and relocating gets a partial discount (based on structure value) toward new bases.

## FAQ — Astro Empires (general)

**What is Astro Empires?**
A strategic MMOG space game playable in a web browser. Players build bases, space fleets, research technologies, fight battles, and form alliances, starting from a home planet and expanding into an empire.

**How can I play?**
All that's required is a web browser and an internet connection.

**What does "persistent universe" mean?**
The game runs 24/7. Whether online or offline, bases and fleets continue performing allocated orders.

**How long will the game last?**
Anticipated long life, with continual improvement; if a restart/closure is ever necessary, as much notice as possible will be given.

**Can I have more than one account?**
Only one account per game server. Players sharing a computer/IP should play different servers or request IP-share permission (subject to rules).

## FAQ — Recyclers

**How to use Recyclers?**
Deploy Recyclers to an astro with debris present. Each Recycler automatically collects 10 credits from the debris field at xx:30 every hour.

**How much debris is left after a unit is destroyed?**
If ships are destroyed in an attack, 40% of the cost of destroyed units converts to debris remaining at the attack location.
